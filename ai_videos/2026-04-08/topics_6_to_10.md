### Narrative

The video opens on a deliberately mundane image: a dimly lit bedroom-office at night. A young person drops into a desk chair, the leather creaking slightly. The desk is cluttered — energy drink cans, sticky notes, a mechanical keyboard with RGB backlighting casting faint color onto their chin. They crack their knuckles, pull on over-ear headphones, and stare at a dark monitor. For a beat, nothing happens. They slump. They fidget. They glance at their phone. The room feels small and oppressive.

Then something shifts. Their fingers find the keyboard and begin to move — slowly at first, then with increasing rhythm. The monitor's glow intensifies, washing their face in blue-white light. As their typing accelerates, the edges of the room begin to soften. The walls lose their texture, becoming translucent, then transparent. Through the dissolving walls, stars appear — first faintly, then brilliantly. The sticky notes on the wall peel away and become drifting points of light.

Now the transformation is complete. The person sits at the same desk, in the same chair, but they float in open space. A nebula swirls slowly behind them in deep violet and teal. The keyboard beneath their fingers emits streams of luminous code — glowing symbols and geometric shapes that arc upward from their fingertips like slow-motion fireworks. Their face is serene, focused, almost meditative. The energy drink can beside them floats weightlessly, catching starlight.

The camera slowly orbits the floating workspace, revealing the scale of the cosmic environment — galaxies in the distance, a planet's edge curving below. The streams of light from their work have become a vast web connecting distant stars, as if their code is building constellations. For a final beat, the camera pushes in close on their face — eyes reflecting galaxies, the faintest smile of someone completely lost in their craft.

A sharp sound — an alarm, a notification. The cosmic scene shatters like glass. We snap back to the mundane room. The person blinks, pulls off their headphones. Morning light leaks through the blinds. They look at the clock: 6:47 AM. They have been working all night. They look back at the screen, and in the reflection of their glasses, we catch one last glimpse of stars.
