**Drift Risks**: Hands are notoriously difficult for AI — finger count and joint angles may drift between shots. Flour particles on skin may appear/disappear inconsistently. The oven's warm glow reflection on his face must stay consistent.
**Mitigation Strategies**: Lock hand poses to simple, stable positions (flat palms on dough, cupped hold). Use close-up crops that show 2–3 fingers rather than full hand spreads. Keep flour distribution consistent via prompt — "light dusting of flour on knuckles and forearms, no flour on face." Generate oven glow as a fixed warm orange overlay (#E8943A) rather than dynamic flame.

### Narrative

The video opens on a pair of weathered hands resting on a flour-dusted wooden countertop. The hands are still for a moment — the knuckles swollen, the skin paper-thin and mapped with veins, a thin band of dough dried under the thumbnail. Then they move, slowly pressing into a mound of pale dough. The bakery around him emerges in the warm half-light of pre-dawn: copper pans hanging from iron hooks, a blackboard menu with prices written in chalk that hasn't been updated in years, a row of empty bread baskets lined with checkered cloth.

The old man lifts the dough and folds it with practiced precision. His face comes into view — deep-set eyes behind wire-frame glasses, a white stubble beard, a canvas apron stained with decades of butter and flour. His expression is calm but heavy, the look of someone performing a ritual for the last time. Behind him, a hand-written sign on the door reads "感谢30年" (Thank you for 30 years) in red ink.

As he shapes the loaf — a round boule, scored with three clean slashes — the light shifts. The pre-dawn blue gives way to golden hour warmth streaming through the bakery window. The scored bread sits on a wooden peel. He slides it into the dark mouth of a brick oven, and as the oven door closes, the warm orange light floods the frame.

The final sequence: the finished loaf sits on the counter, its crust a deep amber, steam rising in slow curls. The baker removes his apron, hangs it on a hook by the door, and turns to look at the bread one last time. The camera holds on his face — a single tear catches the golden light. He smiles. Cut to black.
